Security Review - 3 March 2026
Attendees: Head of Security, IT Lead, Compliance Lead, CTO

1  We opened by reviewing open findings from the last internal audit.
2  Decision: multi-factor authentication is now REQUIRED for all administrative access,
3          including cloud consoles, production servers, and the identity provider.
4  This applies to every admin account with no exceptions; standing exceptions are withdrawn.
5
6  We discussed log handling after the near-miss in February.
7  Decision: security log retention increases from 6 months to 12 months.
8          Retained logs are classified as Confidential and stored in the SIEM only.
9
10 On third parties, two vendors were onboarded without a completed security review.
11 Decision: all new suppliers must complete a security assessment BEFORE any data is shared,
12         and existing critical suppliers are reassessed annually.
13
14 We agreed the acceptable-use rules were out of date on personal devices.
15 Decision: personal (BYOD) devices may access email and calendar only, never production
16         systems or customer data, and must have device encryption enabled.
17
18 Encryption standards were reviewed against current guidance.
19 Decision: data in transit must use TLS 1.2 or higher; TLS 1.0 and 1.1 are disallowed.
20         Data at rest uses AES-256.
21
22 On incident handling, the current 72-hour notification window was felt to be too slow.
23 Decision: confirmed security incidents are reported to the response lead within 1 hour,
24         and notifiable breaches are reported to the regulator within 72 hours.
25
26 We reviewed patching cadence after two overdue critical patches.
27 Decision: critical vulnerabilities are remediated within 7 days, high within 30 days.
28
29 Change management: an emergency change last month bypassed approval.
30 Decision: all production changes require a recorded approval; emergency changes are
31         approved retrospectively within 24 hours.
32
33 Actions: the policy owner to update the affected policies from these decisions and
34         circulate for approval at the next review.
